Hi, I'm Modestas

..a Full stack developer

Over the years, I have met many awesome people of varying aspirations and ambitions, but felt no closer to my own. Eventually after a bout of inspiration and a yearning to try something extraordinary, I signed up to Codecademy. The awe of rendering my first HTML page led to months of binging online tutorials, culminating in my commitment to a coding bootcamp.

Most recently, I have been freelancing remotely for a small startup - building react apps. I'm confident that I've built a solid base from which I am always looking to expand upon. I am eager to level up my skills, reinforce best practices and sink my teeth into new projects as part of a friendly team who are passionate about continuous growth and work life balance.

It really is hard to exaggerate how invaluable learning to code has been for me - just a daily onslaught of confronting my doubts and limitations. It's something that appeases the creative desire I once had as a kid, whilst simultaneously providing an avenue for problem-solving that had previously been lacking in my life. It has taught me through direct experience that there is no substitute for true understanding and cutting corners is just not viable. It has given me some of my most satisfying yet frustrating moments. It is the most rewarding thing I've ever done, and possibly will ever do, as I'm continually humbled through this never-ending process of learning.

Fitness has always given me something to strive for, especially at times where I have felt directionless. Wether it be going for a run or boxing, the flood of endorphins that mother nature rewards you with for your hard work is always a pleasure to look forward to.

I have to praise the ‘Waking up’ app for a brilliant introduction into meditation. Unlike most apps, sessions are centered around recognising the profundity of awareness, which has led to some interesting personal revelations and perhaps the most stark increase in my wellbeing. Despite the mental 'clutter', you really can discover an intrinsic peacefulness to consciousness, simply by paying careful attention; if only for a few moments at a time.

I love a well narrated audiobook. My most captivating listen has been Bill Bryson’s ‘A short history of nearly everything’. It's a fascinating tale of what we know about the universe so far, from beginning to end. After listening to this audiobook, you may feel that much more lucky to be alive.

My absolute favourite podcast is the Joe Rogan Experience. Among many things, Joe is a UFC commentator and stand up comic. He gets guests on from all walks of life to discuss anything from their passions to perspectives. some of my favourite episodes have been with the eloquent astrophysicist - Neil Degrasse Tyson, and Boyan Slat - A young entrepreneur pioneering ‘The Ocean Cleanup’ project.

Euphoria

MERN stack

For my final GA project, I created an app that retrieves concert data from the Skiddle API and allows users to track all of the concerts they want to attend in London. They can filter and sort the gigs by parameters such as name and date. I also used the Google Maps API, to display the precise location of any given concert.

ICON

React, Redux, Firebase, Node.js

Icon is a web app for folks in the energy business. Subscribers can view insights uploaded by the admin, participate in a forum, schedule events in a calendar or upload a legal register to check their compliance with the law. I joined this project over a year ago, helping to maintain the current codebase and build new features.

Thingdex

Expo, Firebase, Node.js

Thingdex is an app that allows users to save and recommend links on the internet. You can enter (almost) any link, and its metadata (such as image and title) will be parsed and stored in one place so you can have a catalogue of all of your favourite things.

Chimneys & Candy Canes

HTML5, CSS3, JavaScript (ES6), jQuery

This was my first Frontend project which involved building a JavaScript game. Since it was the holiday season, I decided to make a festive version of snakes and ladders; utilising all of the fundamental concepts of JavaScript such as functions, arrays, loops and objects. jQuery is also used for DOM manipulation.

Superfoods

Node.js MongoDB, Mongoose, Express.js, EJS, Bootstrap 4, SCSS, JavaScript

This was my first full-stack RESTful application utilising all CRUD functionalities and using EJS templates to render server-side HTML. The premise was to create an index of foods with great macronutrients which users can search through, favourite or upload their own.

SPLTN

MEAN stack

This app involved working directly with two of my classmates at GA. We built and presented this Full Stack Application that allows groups of individuals to log a trip and split the bills on that trip, calculating how much is owed between everyone. We used the Google Maps and Chart.js API to accomplish this.
