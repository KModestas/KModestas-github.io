Hi, I'm Modestas

..A developer (currently) based in London

Over the years, I have met many great people of varying aspirations and ambitions, but felt no closer to my own. This was until realising that I needed to stop limiting my options to those that I was capable of at that specific point in time. The liberating realisation that I can learn anything that I set my mind to, has cultivated my desire to code.

After doing some self learning at home through various online courses and youtube tutorials; I decided to fully commit by enrolling in GA’s WDI course. As anticipated, coding has not been an easy endeavor; though not as intimidating as my preconceptions led me to believe.

As of late, I have been freelancing as a developer whilst working from home. I feel much more confident in coming up with new ideas and building new features and have for the most part beaten my imposter syndrome. The biggest lessons I have learned so far as a developer are that once you overcome your doubt, you can literally learn anything; and an error is not a negative thing, but merely a tool that guides you through the development of your application.

Fitness has always given me something to strive for, especially at times where I have felt directionless. Wether it be going for a run or boxing, at the end of each session; the flood of endorphins that mother nature rewards you with for your hard work is amazing to say the least.

My absolute favourite podcast is the Joe Rogan Experience. Among many things, Joe is a UFC commentator and stand up comic. He gets guests on from all walks of life to discuss anything from their passions to their perspectives. To say this podcast has changed my life, would be a massive understatement. The podcast linked below was the trigger that started my journey 3 years ago in trying to combine a fulfilling craft like coding with travel in an attempt to cultivate a lifestyle that is true to me.

I love a good audio book, particularly if it’s narrated well. One of the most fascinating and intriguiding listens for me has been Bill Bryson’s ‘A short history of nearly everything’. It is a miraculous tale of what we know about the universe till this present day. After listening to this audiobook, you will feel lucky to be alive.

I have been regularly meditating using the ‘Waking Up’ App. Sessions focus primarily on recognising the profound nature of consciousness which has led to some interesting personal revelations and prehaps the most signficant increase in my quality of life so far. No matter the contents of your mind, you can always discover that amongst all the mental clutter, there is an intrinsic peacefulness to consciousness. It's up to you to notice it.

Euphoria

MERN stack

For my final GA project, I created an app that retrieves concert data from the skiddle API and allows users to track all of the concerts they want to go to in London. They can filter and sort the gigs by parameters such as name and date. I also used the google Maps API, to display the precise location of any given concert.

ICON

React, Redux, Firebase, Node.js

Icon is a web app for folks in the energy business. Subscribers can view insights uploaded by the admin, participate in a forumn, schedule events in a calendar or upload a legal register to check their compliance with the law. I joined this project a year ago, helping to maintain the current codebase and build new features.

thingdex

Expo, Firebase, Node.js

thingdex is an app I am currently working on that allows users to save and recommend links on the internet! You can enter (almost) any link, and its metadata (such as image and title) will be parsed and stored in one place so you can have a catalogue of all of your favourite things.

Chimneys & Candy Canes

HTML5, CSS3, JavaScript (ES6), jQuery

This was my first Front End project which involved building a JavaScript game. Since it was the holiday season, I decided to make a festive version of snakes and ladders which utilised all the fundamental concepts of JavaScript such as (but not limited to) functions, array, loops and objects.

Superfoods

Node.js MongoDB, Mongoose, Express.js, EJS, Bootstrap 4, SCSS, JavaScript

This was my first Full-stack application which involved building a fully RESTful application containing all CRUD functionalities. I built an app displaying an index of foods with their macronutrients which users can upload or find after registering and logging in.

SPLTN

MEAN stack

This app involved working directly with two of my classmates at GA. We built a Full Stack Application that allows groups of individuals to log a trip and split the bills on that trip so that each person pays the same. We used the Google Maps and Chart.js API to accomplish this.
